## Submission not showing in “new”?

HN’s “new” page is **https://news.ycombinator.com/newest**. If your post doesn’t appear there:

1. **Check your profile**  
   Go to `https://news.ycombinator.com/submitted?id=YOUR_USERNAME`. If the submission is there, it was accepted. It might still be missing from `/newest` for others (see below).

2. **Enable “showdead”**  
   In [your HN profile](https://news.ycombinator.com/settings), turn on **showdead**. Reload `/newest` or your submission page. If you now see your post with **[dead]** next to it, it was killed by software, user flags, or a moderator. You can’t unkill it yourself; someone with enough karma can **vouch** for it (link appears on the submission page when showdead is on).

3. **New or low-karma accounts**  
   HN sometimes holds or downranks submissions from very new accounts or domains to limit spam. The post may exist but not be visible to others on `/newest`, or it may appear only for you.

4. **Don’t resubmit**  
   Submitting the same URL again will be treated as a duplicate. Don’t delete and resubmit.

5. **Ask the moderators**  
   If you’re sure you’re on `/newest`, the post isn’t in your profile, and it’s not [dead] with showdead on, email **hn@ycombinator.com** with your username and the submission URL. They can say whether it was filtered and sometimes restore posts.

### New account?

HN often filters or hides submissions from brand‑new accounts to limit spam. That’s probably why yours didn’t show.

- **Email the mods:** Write to **hn@ycombinator.com**. Say you’re a new user, that it’s your first submission and your own article, and paste the link to the submission (from your profile) and the article URL. They sometimes unkill or allow a first submission when the content is clearly on-topic.
- **Build karma, then try again later:** Use HN normally for a few weeks—comment where you have something useful to add, don’t ask for upvotes. Once you have a bit of karma, future submissions are less likely to be filtered. You can submit a *different* post later; don’t delete and resubmit the same one unless a mod says it’s OK.
- **If someone else finds it and submits it:** If a reader (e.g. from LinkedIn or Reddit) finds your post useful and submits it themselves, that’s within the guidelines. You can’t ask for that or coordinate upvotes—that’s against the rules.
